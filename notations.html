Basic Face Moves

Each letter refers to a face of the cube. A bare letter means turn that face 90° clockwise when looking directly at it.

U
Up
Top face, clockwise from above
D
Down
Bottom face, clockwise from below
F
Front
Face facing you, clockwise
B
Back
Face away from you, clockwise from behind
L
Left
Left face, clockwise from left
R
Right
Right face, clockwise from right
Modifiers

Suffixes change what a basic move does. You'll see these constantly in algorithms.

(none)
Clockwise 90°
E.g. R — right face, one quarter turn clockwise
'
Counter-clockwise
E.g. R' — right face, one quarter turn the other way
2
Double (180°)
E.g. F2 — front face, half turn (same both ways)
💡 A quick trick: R U R' U' is one of the most common four-move sequences in cube-solving. Read it as: Right clockwise, Up clockwise, Right counter-clockwise, Up counter-clockwise.
Key Algorithms

These are the core move sequences (algorithms / "algs") used to solve the cube. Memorising even a few of these goes a long way.

Name Moves What it does
Sexy Move
R U R' U'
Fundamental 4-move sequence. Cycles three corners.
OLL Cross
F R U R' U' F'
Forms a yellow cross on the bottom face.
Sune
R U R' U R U2 R'
Orients last layer corners. One of the most used OLL algs.
U-Perm
R U R' U R U2 R'
Cycles three last-layer edges (PLL).
Corner Swap
U R U' L' U R' U' L
Swaps last-layer corners without disturbing edges.
Right Insert
U R U' R'
Inserts an edge piece into the right slot (F2L).
Left Insert
U' L' U L
Inserts an edge piece into the left slot (F2L).
Reading Tips
Hold your cube consistently. White on top, orange in front — always. If you rotate the whole cube, the letters change meaning.
Clockwise means clockwise from that face's perspective. For R, stand to the right of the cube and look at it, that's the clockwise direction for R.
Algorithms are repeatable. If something goes wrong, repeat the algorithm — most algs cycle back to the start after 2–6 repetitions.
Do the moves slowly first. Speed comes naturally. Understanding what each move does matters more than how fast you do it.
B moves are awkward. B means the back face, clockwise from behind. Many beginners re-orient the cube so B becomes F.
